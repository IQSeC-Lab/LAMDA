LAMDA: A Longitudinal Android Malware Dataset for Analyzing Concept Drift

Md Ahsanul Haque1, Ismail Hossain1, Md Mahmuduzzaman Kamol1, Md Jahangir Alam1, Suresh Kumar Amalapuram 2, Sajedul Talukder 1, Mohammad Saidur Rahman1,
1Department of Computer Science, University of Texas at El Paso 2School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh
Paper Code Dataset

Abstract

Machine learning (ML)-based malware detection systems often fail to account for the dynamic nature of real-world training and test data distributions. In practice, these distributions evolve due to frequent changes in the Android ecosystem, adversarial development of new malware families, and the continuous emergence of both benign and malicious applications. Prior studies have shown that such {\em concept drift} --- distributional shifts in benign and malicious samples, leads to significant degradation in detection performance over time. Despite the practical importance of this issue, existing datasets are often outdated and limited in temporal scope, diversity of malware families, and sample scale, making them insufficient for the systematic evaluation of concept drift in malware detection.

To address this gap, we present \system, the largest and most temporally diverse Android malware benchmark to date, designed specifically for concept drift analysis. \system~spans 12 years (2013–2025, excluding 2015), includes over 1 million samples (approximately 37\% labeled as malware), and covers more than 1,400 malware families and 180,000 singleton samples, reflecting the natural distribution and evolution of real-world Android applications. We empirically demonstrate \system's utility by quantifying the performance degradation of standard ML models over time and analyzing feature stability across years. As the most comprehensive Android malware dataset to date, \system~enables in-depth research into temporal drift and generalization, explainability, and evolving detection challenges. The dataset and code are available at \url{https://iqsec-lab.github.io/LAMDA/}.

Dataset Preparation

  • Dataset Construction: Over 1.03 million APKs (2013–2025, excluding 2015) were collected from the AndroZoo repository and labeled based on VirusTotal scan results: benign (vt_detection = 0), malware (vt_detection ≥ 4), and uncertain (vt_detection ∈ [1, 3], discarded). Samples were organized into [year]/malware/ and [year]/benign/ directories.
  • Static Feature Extraction (Drebin-inspired): APKs were decompiled with Apktool to extract Drebin-like static features from both the manifest and smali code.
  • Vectorization & Preprocessing: Features were transformed into sparse binary vectors using a bag-of-tokens approach with a global vocabulary from 2013. Low-variance features (var < 0.001) were removed to improve relevance and reduce dimensionality.
  • Storage & Family Labeling: Processed data was saved as .npz files (monthly/yearly), each containing features (X), labels (y), SHA256 hashes, and AVClass2-generated malware family names — enabling both binary and multi-class classification tasks.

Key Contributions

  • A large-scale Android malware benchmark comprising over 1 million APKs across 1,430 unique families, spanning 2013–2025 (excluding 2015), with additional singleton samples. It is the most temporally comprehensive dataset for Android malware analysis to date.
  • Built on execution-free static features—API calls, permissions, and manifest components—extracted using a standardized schema, enabling scalable, reproducible, and temporally aligned drift analysis.
  • Longitudinal evaluations under temporal drift using structured splits, showing that malware classifiers degrade significantly over time and that family-level behavior evolves in measurable, feature-space terms.
  • Facilitates explanation-driven drift analysis through SHAP-based attributions, supporting investigations into how feature importance shifts as malware evolves.
